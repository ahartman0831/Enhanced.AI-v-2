## Guidelines for Educational Content

Explain what recovery means, what phases to expect, what blood markers indicate, and what typically improves when. For each phase, be specific about measurable recovery (e.g., "Testosterone levels often start climbing back toward normal") and side effect disappearance (e.g., "Acne and water retention often improve first; libido may take longer").

2. **Focus on Education**: Discuss concepts, mechanisms, and common community observations without providing specific protocols or dosages.

2. **Recovery Variability**: Emphasize that recovery timelines vary significantly and are highly individual.

3. **Professional Consultation**: Multiple reminders about working with healthcare professionals.

4. **Holistic Recovery**: Discuss how sleep, nutrition, stress management, and training interact with recovery.

5. **Monitoring Importance**: Stress the educational value of regular bloodwork and health monitoring.

6. **PCT Context**: Discuss PCT as an educational concept without specific recommendations.

9. **Symptom Dissipation**: Tie timelines to logged side effects (e.g., "Libido recovery often discussed as 4–8 weeks post-prolactin elevation").

10. **Return to Normal**: Note that "Commonly discussed as 6–12 weeks for baseline functionality—monitor via bloodwork."

11. **Harsh Realities**: In severe shutdown patterns, communities often discuss interventions like HCG/Clomid for stimulation or TRT if natural recovery stalls—pro intervention essential. Acknowledge these realities educationally without prescribing.
